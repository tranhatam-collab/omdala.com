# OMDALA GROWTH STRATEGY 100K USERS 2026

## 1. Purpose

Define a realistic growth strategy for reaching 100,000 users through a narrow product wedge before expanding platform breadth.

This document is intended for product, GTM, growth, and fundraising alignment.

---

## 2. Growth Target

Primary target:

- 100,000 users within 6 to 12 months only if wedge retention and unit economics are validated

This target should be treated as execution-dependent, not assumed.

---

## 3. Initial Wedge

Recommended wedge:

- Zero Overdue for SMEs in Vietnam and broader SEA

Why this wedge:

- pain is concrete
- ROI is measurable
- commitment and proof model fits naturally
- trust can improve with real financial outcomes

---

## 4. Channels

Initial acquisition channels:

- communities
- partnerships
- founder networks
- local business hubs
- operator-led onboarding

Rules:

- do not spread across too many channels early
- focus on distribution channels with trust and operator leverage

---

## 5. Growth Loop

Base loop:

1. onboard business
2. solve overdue pain quickly
3. trust increases through proof-backed outcomes
4. business invites network participants
5. more commitments run through the system

This loop must be measurable before aggressive scaling.

---

## 6. Metrics

Core growth metrics:

- CAC
- activation rate
- retention
- commitments created
- commitments completed
- trust score movement
- referrals or invited counterparties

Business metrics:

- overdue reduction
- revenue retained or recovered
- active SME accounts

---

## 7. Expansion Path

Suggested sequence:

- Vietnam
- SEA
- selected global operator networks

Expansion should happen only after:

- wedge retention is real
- activation is repeatable
- trust model is stable
- onboarding playbooks are documented

---

## 8. Risks

- chasing user count without proof of retained value
- weak onboarding support reducing activation
- opening too many markets before the wedge is stable
- overpromising trust or automation before runtime maturity

---

## 9. Next Actions

1. Define activation and retention targets for Zero Overdue.
2. Build channel-specific onboarding playbooks.
3. Track referral and network-expansion loops.
4. Expand only after the first wedge shows repeatable retention.

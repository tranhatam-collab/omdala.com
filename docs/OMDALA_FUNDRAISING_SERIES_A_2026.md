# OMDALA FUNDRAISING SERIES A 2026

## 1. Purpose

Define a fundraising strategy that matches OMDALA's actual stage, wedge execution, and long-term infrastructure narrative.

This document is intended for founder planning and investor preparation.

---

## 2. Stage Path

Recommended progression:

- Pre-Seed
- Seed
- Series A

Each stage should be tied to real system proof, not only conceptual ambition.

---

## 3. Series A Target

Indicative Series A target:

- 10M USD to 30M USD

This range depends on:

- real wedge traction
- retention quality
- revenue evidence
- credibility of infrastructure path

---

## 4. Use of Funds

Primary use of funds:

- engineering
- product
- growth
- operator and trust workflows
- infrastructure hardening

Capital should support runtime truth, not only narrative expansion.

---

## 5. Core Story

Fundraising story:

- OMDALA is an infrastructure layer for verified coordination
- the company begins with narrow wedges and reusable system primitives
- trust, proof, commitments, and transitions create a long-term moat

The story must stay grounded in what is actually live and measurable.

---

## 6. Metrics Needed Before Series A

Recommended proof points:

- ARR above 1M USD or equivalent strong monetization signal
- active users or active operators with repeat behavior
- retention quality
- commitment throughput growth
- proven overdue reduction or similar wedge-specific ROI

Growth quality matters more than vanity reach.

---

## 7. Investor Fit

Most relevant investor categories:

- AI funds
- infrastructure funds
- fintech or workflow infrastructure investors
- operators who understand trust, compliance, and execution systems

Avoid pitching pure infrastructure ambition without wedge proof.

---

## 8. Timeline

Recommended Series A timing:

- 6 to 12 months after MVP only if retention, monetization, and system proof are strong enough

Do not force fundraising timing ahead of runtime proof.

---

## 9. Risks

- raising on category ambition without enough product truth
- overstating traction
- confusing global vision with current operating maturity

---

## 10. Next Actions

1. Define stage-appropriate fundraising milestones.
2. Tie investor narrative to actual product metrics.
3. Build a metrics-backed Series A deck only after wedge proof is visible.
4. Keep the infrastructure vision, but lead with runtime truth.
